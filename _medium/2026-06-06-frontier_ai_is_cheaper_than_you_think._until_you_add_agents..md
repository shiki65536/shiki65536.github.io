Two weeks ago, I wrote about why AI tokens have become more expensive in 2026. This week, I wanted to dig into a different question:If I use Sonnet-class AI heavily every day, what’s actually the cheapest way to buy intelligence?Many people assume:Dashboard AI is cheapestAPIs are expensiveLocal AI eventually wins on costAfter running the numbers, I reached a different conclusion.AssumptionsLet’s assume an AI power user:300 requests per day2,000 input tokens per request500 output tokens per requestMonthly usage:18M input tokens4.5M output tokens22.5M total tokensModel capability is benchmarked against Claude Sonnet 4.6.1. Dashboard AIExamples, Claude Pro, ChatGPT Plus, Gemini Pro. On paper:$20–30/monthThis is easily the cheapest option.However: dashboard products are not designed for high-volume workloads.Once you need:automationagent workflowsbackend integrationssubscriptions stop being a true alternative to APIs.2. Direct API AccessExamples: Claude API, GPT API, Gemini API.Using Sonnet 4.6 pricing: Input: $54/month, Output: $67.50/month.Total: ~$122/monthIn reality, once you add:RAGTool callingMemorycosts typically rise to: $120–450/month3. AWS AgentCoreOriginally, I assumed running agents on AWS would be significantly more expensive than calling APIs directly. After looking at AgentCore pricing, I found the opposite.For a lightweight developer setup:AgentCore RuntimeBedrockMCP ToolsBasic loggingonly add a few dollars per month.The expensive part is still the model. For most developer workloads: ~$125–140/monthThe cost explosion begins when agents start, token consumption still becomes the dominant cost.4. Local AIExamples: Ollama, vLLM, Qwen, LlamaAlthough I still doubt whether their can perform as well as Claude Sonnet 4.6.Assuming:RTX 5090 workstation~$4,000 GPU3-year depreciationplus:electricitystorageRAMthe effective cost is roughly: $100–450/month, depending on hardware configuration.Monthly Cost Comparison+---------------+--------------+|    Option     | Monthly Cost |+---------------+--------------+| Dashboard AI  | $20–30*      || API           | $120–450     || AWS AgentCore | $125–500     || Local AI      | $100–450     |+---------------+--------------+* Subject to usage limits.The Most Interesting FindingI originally assumed local AI would be the obvious winner.It wasn’t.For most engineers, frontier AI is still surprisingly cheap to rent.The real cost isn’t the token.It’s the agent.A 2026 study found:Agentic coding tasks can consume up to 1,000× more tokens than a normal chat interactionThe same task can vary by 30× in costMore tokens do not necessarily produce better resultsIn other words: The cost explosion often comes not from model pricing, but from asking AI to plan, search, validate, and execute work autonomously.ConclusionThe biggest takeaway from this exercise wasn’t: AI is expensive.It was: Frontier intelligence is cheaper than I expected.At least in mid 2026:Dashboard AI remains the cheapest way to consume intelligenceAPIs provide the best balance of capability and costAWS AgentCore makes sense when automation and integration matterLocal AI becomes attractive only at higher volumes or when privacy is a requirementAs agentic systems become more common, the metric worth tracking may no longer be: Cost per Token, but: Cost per Completed Task.ReferencesAnthropic PricingAWS AgentCore PricingHow Do AI Agents Spend Your Money? (2026)Local AI Cost Assumptions- RTX 5090 Australian retail pricing (Scorptec, PLE Computers)- Australian residential electricity pricing (AER)
