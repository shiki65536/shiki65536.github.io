My friend argued that GenAI is a commodity, so token prices will inevitably plummet. I countered that computing requires electricity; without a leap in energy tech (like nuclear fusion), slashing token prices while remaining profitable is structurally impossible.Looking at the mid-2026 reality, the “subsidy era” has ended. AI companies are finally crashing into the wall of unit economics.1. The Setup: The “LLMflation” Illusion (2023–2025)Hyperscalers aggressively subsidized tokens and context windows to capture market share.VCs coined the term ”LLMflation.” Academic papers showed the price halving-time for economic models was just 1.1 years — far outpacing hardware.Many mistook this predatory pricing for true cost efficiency. It wasn’t. It was a massive burn rate disguised as a tech shift.2. The Crash: The Cash Flow Reality (2025–2026)Under pressure to prove profitability, Claude, OpenAI, Gemini, and GitHub Copilot overhavled pricing into “compute-based” quotas, causing a real-world cost increase of 8x to 33x for power users.Uber burned its entire 2026 AI token budget in just 4 months.Microsoft departments deprecated Claude Code by June 30 due to unfeasible compute overheads.3. The Root Cause: Why AI is Not SaaSMany analogize AI to cloud storage or SaaS. This is a fundamental flaw.Storage is “write-once, idle-cheap.”LLMs are *tateless and compute-heavy on every single interaction. Every prompt requires re-computing millions of parameters.Three drivers are blowing up the bills:The Reasoning Premium: Frontier models rely on Chain-of-Thought. A simple response triggers thousands of “hidden reasoning tokens,” making them 31.5x more expensive than standard ones.Agentic AI: Moving from “searching Google” (hundreds of tokens) to coding agents (millions of tokens) scales consumption up to 1,000x.The Stochastic Trap: Token usage is highly stochastic. The same task can vary 30x in cost, yet more tokens do not guarantee better accuracy ([arXiv:2604.22750]).The Physics Constraint: Per IEA data, data center power usage jumped 17% in 2025, driven by AI. By 2030, AI-specific data center power demand will triple.4. The Path to “Cheap Tokens” &amp; The Jevons ParadoxHow do we make tokens cheaper?Algorithmic Efficiency: Distillation cuts inference energy by 50–90%, but triggers Jevons Paradox ([Global Electricity Data]): lower costs unlock massive deployments, causing _total_ energy consumption to skyrocket.Hardware Evolution: Modern AI processors are 99% more efficient than 2008 models, but workload scale outpaces hardware gains ([IAEI Magazine]).Energy Sovereignty: Big Tech isn’t waiting for the grid; they are buying nuclear power revivals and building proprietary power infrastructure.ConclusionAI is not a lightweight software play; it is an industrial system rigidly constrained by physical energy. Algorithmic efficiency is fast, but it is locked in a direct race against the soaring costs of the power grid and hardware amortization.Without a baseline energy breakthrough like nuclear fusion, infinite, dirt-cheap AI tokens remain a physical impossibility.References[1] Academic Paper on Pricing Dynamics: [Tiered Super-Moore’s Law: Price Evolution, Production Frontiers, and Market Competition in Large Language Model Inference Services(arXiv, 2026)][2] Tech Infrastructure Analysis: [AI Is Too Expensive_ — Ed Zitron’s Where’s Your Ed At (2026)][3] Enterprise Budget &amp; Revenue Race: [The AI Token Pricing Crisis Behind OpenAI and Anthropic’s Revenue Race (Investing.com, 2026)][4] Data Center &amp; Energy Trends:Data Centers Energy Consumption &amp; The Jevons Paradox (Global Electricity Council / IEA Metrics, 2026)[5] Hardware Efficiency Metrics: How Much Electricity Does a Data Center Use?
